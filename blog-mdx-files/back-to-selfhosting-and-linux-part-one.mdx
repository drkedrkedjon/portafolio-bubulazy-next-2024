> After some twenty or so years, I am back to using Linux, VPS, and self-hosting. I started in 1997 or so with Red Hat 4.0 on a generic beige Pentium box, and I left in 2006 once I realised that the year of Linux on the desktop had not yet arrived. And it still hasn't. I went the Apple way...

## Intro

I became curious about Linux in 1997. Generic beige box with CPU frequency on display... Pirated copy of Windows 95... So the curiosity became a tiny obsession and I was going Windows/Linux for a year, installing and reinstalling both of them. Neither of which made me happy. Unfortunately, that Pentium CPU and RAM were too low for Red Hat & OpenOffice, so in 1998 I went Gentoo. Bit better but still too slow.

So I got myself a new grey Dell box, but the graphics card was too modern, so I had to wait until May of 2000 to get driver support, and since that day up to November of 2006 I was a full Gentoo user. Compiling all the shit you can compile and fiddling with tiny compiler adjustments for optimised code.

Then the obsession of staring at the terminal screen while updating and compiling became less and less interesting and boredom entered. At the same time I started to make serious/important files (photography and video). So, I have started to hate Linux, not for being Linux, but for too much code and too little usability, especially on desktop apps.

In 2006, comparing the desktop apps usability between Gentoo to OSX was like heaven and hell, so I went Apple way. And no Windows, not even in hell.
